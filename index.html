Pillars of "Bangladesh 2.0"

Innovation

Bangladesh performs better in innovation outputs than innovation inputs in 2024. This year Bangladesh ranks 114th in innovation inputs. This position is the same as last year.

Sustainability

Bangladesh is also home to a burgeoning manufacturing sector, which lifts people out of poverty, but can also contribute to environmental, health, and human rights issues for workers and surrounding communities.

Infrastructure

Bangladesh has been developing its infrastructure to improve connectivity and economic growth. This includes building roads, bridges, railways, ports, and power plants, as well as improving rural infrastructure.

Empowerment

Bangladesh has made remarkable progress in the last 20 years in improving the lives of women and girls.The maternal mortality rate has decreased by more than two-thirds since 2000 and continues to fall.

The Vision of Future

Bangladesh 2.0 aims to redefine the nation's trajectory by embracing cutting-edge technology, promoting inclusive growth, and ensuring sustainability. With initiatives in renewable energy, digital transformation, and robust infrastructure, the country is set to become a global model of development.

Digital Transformation

Creating a nationwide digital ecosystem to enhance connectivity and access to services for all citizens.

Green Energy

Investments in solar and wind energy to ensure a sustainable and eco-friendly future.

Modern Infrastructure

Building state-of-the-art infrastructure to support economic growth and global competitiveness.

"This is a crucial moment for Bangladesh. The vision of a new, inclusive, and just society is within reach, but it demands collective effort and a clear path forward.The 'country as a family' metaphor, while evocative, may not fully capture the diverse realities and challenges facing the nation. Unity is essential, but it must also respect and embrace diversity. Citizens are not just looking for unity; they want a sense of autonomy and inclusivity within the broader national framework."

Dr. Muhammad Yunus

Chief Adviser of the People's Republic of Bangladesh
